Chapter 01

What is Artificial Intelligence?

Imagine traditional computer programs as a strict cooking recipe. If the computer follows the recipe steps exactly, it makes a cake. But if something unexpected happens, the program crashes.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is different. Instead of a strict recipe, you show the computer 1,000 pictures of cakes and let it learn the patterns of what a cake looks like on its own!

AI finds repeating mathematical patterns to solve complex tasks

Chapter 02

The AI Capability Spectrum

AI isn't just one single technology. It is grouped into three distinct milestones based on how adaptable it is:

The AI Evolution Scale

1
ANI
2
AGI
3
ASI

Narrow AI (ANI)

The "One-Trick Pony" (Present Day)

This is the only type of AI that exists today. It is incredibly good at one specific task, like identifying suspicious credit card activity or recommending your next song on Spotify. However, a chess AI cannot read a resume, and ChatGPT cannot drive a car.

Real-World Availability 100% (Everyday Use)

Real Examples you use today:

Google Maps Navigation Netflix Suggestions

General AI (AGI)

The "Human Brain" (Not Invented Yet)

A hypothetical machine that has a full, flexible brain. It could learn to play guitar, understand economics, solve coding bugs, and adapt to *any* situation exactly like a human being, using the exact same underlying logic model.

Real-World Availability 0% (In Research Labs Only)

Estimated Arrival Timeline

Vigorous Debate (5 to 30 years)

Super AI (ASI)

The "Omniscient Machine" (Sci-Fi)

The theoretical peak where a computer's capability leaps ahead of the collective human race. It wouldn't just write emails; it would instantly solve unsolved physics equations, predict planetary challenges, and design its own descendants.

Real-World Availability 0% (Pure Theory & Fiction)

Current Status

Philosophical Concept Only
